Permission to drive or travel with a vehicle

Vehicle Use Authorization Guide

A vehicle use authorization gives a named driver permission to use, operate, or travel with a vehicle during a defined period and under stated conditions.

What is a vehicle use authorization?

A vehicle use authorization is a written permission from an owner or authorized controller allowing another person to use or travel with a vehicle. The document can identify the driver, vehicle, permitted dates, geographic area, purpose, insurance information, and restrictions. It may help explain that the driver has permission when the owner is not present.

The authorization does not transfer ownership, replace a driver’s license, create insurance coverage, or override a lease, loan, rental agreement, employer policy, or law. The driver and owner remain responsible for confirming all separate requirements.

Common situations where it is used

Families may authorize a relative to borrow a car for travel, school, work, or an emergency. Employers may permit an employee to operate a company vehicle. Owners may allow a caregiver, friend, transporter, or mechanic to use the vehicle for a limited purpose. A written authorization may also be requested for domestic or international travel when the registered owner is not present.

Rental companies, lenders, insurers, employers, border officials, and foreign authorities may require their own documents and may prohibit certain drivers or destinations. A private letter cannot change those contracts or policies.

Who typically uses it?

The signer should be the titled or registered owner, lessee, employer, rental company, fleet manager, or another person with authority to permit use. The driver should be named exactly as shown on their license or passport. Joint ownership, leases, loans, company rules, or rental contracts may require additional permission.

Information commonly included

Owner, driver, and vehicle

Include the owner’s and driver’s full legal names, addresses, contact information, relationship, and identification details when requested. List the vehicle’s year, make, model, color, VIN, license plate, registration state, and insurance information. Avoid placing unnecessary sensitive numbers in a document that will be widely shared.

Dates, geography, purpose, and restrictions

State when permission begins and ends, permitted states or countries, the purpose of use, whether passengers or towing are allowed, mileage limits, and any prohibited activities. For cross-border travel, include destination and return dates and ask whether a separate owner authorization, insurance card, title copy, rental letter, or customs document is required.

Vehicle permission compared with ownership transfer

Permission to use a vehicle does not sell, gift, or transfer title. A vehicle bill of sale documents a purchase, and a vehicle gift affidavit may support a gift transaction. A vehicle release authorization serves a different purpose by allowing retrieval from a holder. Use the document that matches the actual transaction.

Insurance, lender, rental, and border considerations

The owner should contact the insurer to confirm whether the driver and planned use are covered. A financed or leased vehicle may have travel or driver restrictions. Rental contracts commonly prohibit unauthorized drivers. Employers may require training, motor-vehicle checks, or internal approval. Border agencies can request proof of ownership, permission, insurance, travel plans, and identification.

A notarized letter may be helpful but does not create coverage or override any restriction. Confirm requirements with every relevant organization before travel.

Is notarization required?

Notarization is not required for every vehicle-use letter. An insurer, lender, border agency, foreign authority, employer, rental company, or other recipient may request it as an identity safeguard. Requirements vary by jurisdiction, destination, ownership arrangement, and purpose.

Ask whether an acknowledgment, witness, original paper signature, or specific form is required. Notarization verifies the signer and notarial act; it does not confirm insurance, ownership rights, or driving eligibility.

Can it be notarized online?

Remote online notarization may be available when the signer and document qualify and the intended recipient accepts an electronic notarization. The owner completes identity verification, appears by live video, and signs electronically during the session.

Identification commonly needed

The owner signing the authorization generally needs an acceptable, unexpired government-issued photo ID. The driver should carry a valid driver’s license and may need a passport for international travel. Supporting records can include registration, proof of insurance, title or lease information, rental or employer permission, itinerary, and contact information for the owner.

Important acceptance limitations

A private authorization may not satisfy an insurer, lender, rental company, employer, border agency, or foreign authority. The driver may still be denied use or entry because of licensing, insurance, ownership, contract, or destination restrictions. SafeNotarize does not determine driving eligibility or insurance coverage and does not provide legal advice. Confirm current requirements before the vehicle is used or taken across a border.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Failing to list the owner, driver, VIN, plate, dates, destinations, and purpose.
  • Ignoring insurance, lender, lease, rental, employer, or border restrictions.
  • Using a permission letter for a sale, gift, or permanent transfer.
  • Assuming notarization adds the driver to an insurance policy.

Frequently asked questions

What does a vehicle use authorization allow?

It gives a named driver permission to use or travel with a specified vehicle during the dates, places, purposes, and conditions stated in the document.

Does the letter add the driver to my insurance?

No. Contact the insurer to confirm coverage. A permission letter does not create or expand insurance coverage.

Can the driver cross a state or national border with it?

The letter may help show permission, but border agencies, insurers, lenders, and rental companies may require additional records or their own forms.

Is this the same as transferring ownership?

No. It permits use and does not sell, gift, or transfer title to the driver.

Should the letter include an expiration date?

Yes. Include clear start and end dates and any geographic, mileage, passenger, towing, or activity limits.

Does vehicle use authorization always need notarization?

No. Requirements vary by recipient, destination, ownership, and purpose. Confirm whether notarization or an original signature is requested.

Can it be notarized online?

It may be possible when remote online notarization is permitted and the intended recipient accepts an electronic document and seal.
