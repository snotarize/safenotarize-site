Financial support affidavit guide

Affidavit of Support

An affidavit of support is a signed statement in which one person explains the financial or practical support they provide, or intend to provide, for another person.

What is an affidavit of support?

An affidavit of support is a written statement describing a person’s promise, ability, or history of providing financial support to someone else. Depending on the purpose, it may identify the supporter, the person receiving support, their relationship, the amount or type of assistance, and the expected duration. The statement may be used for housing, education, travel, immigration-related files, family matters, or another administrative request.

“Affidavit of support” can also refer to official government forms with detailed eligibility and evidence rules. A general affidavit is not a substitute for an official form. The signer should first identify the exact document requested and use the agency’s current version when one is required.

When is it used?

A private support affidavit may be requested to explain who pays living expenses, housing, tuition, travel, medical costs, or other needs. A landlord, school, consulate, court, benefits office, or private organization may ask for a signed statement and proof of the supporter’s resources.

Government programs may use their own forms and instructions. Those forms can ask for tax records, household information, employment history, or legally binding commitments. SafeNotarize can notarize eligible signatures but does not prepare immigration filings, determine sponsorship eligibility, or certify that financial information is sufficient.

Who typically needs it?

Supporters may include parents, spouses, relatives, employers, sponsors, hosts, or other responsible adults. The person receiving support may be a student, family member, tenant, visitor, applicant, or dependent. An organization may request a separate affidavit from each supporter when more than one person contributes.

Is notarization legally required?

Not every support letter or official support form requires notarization. Some agencies accept a signed declaration under penalty of perjury, while others require a sworn affidavit, acknowledgment, witness, or specific notarial certificate. The document instructions control.

If the recipient asks for a notarized affidavit, the signer must complete the correct notarial act. For a jurat, the signer generally swears or affirms that the statement is true and signs in the notary’s presence. For an acknowledgment, the signer confirms the signature and voluntary execution. The notary cannot select the act when the document or recipient has not specified it.

Can an affidavit of support be notarized online?

Many privately prepared affidavits can be notarized through a remote online session when the governing rules and receiving organization allow it. The signer completes identity verification, appears on live video, and signs electronically under the notary’s direction.

Official agencies, courts, consulates, and foreign recipients can have separate rules. Some may require an original, a specific form, a local notary, or additional authentication after notarization. Confirm the submission requirements before booking. SafeNotarize cannot guarantee acceptance by a third party.

Identification and evidence to prepare

The signer should have an acceptable unexpired government-issued photo ID and access to the final affidavit. The name in the document should match the signer’s legal identity. The online session also requires a camera, microphone, internet connection, and email access.

The recipient may request pay statements, tax records, employment letters, bank statements, housing records, or proof of relationship. These supporting items are not automatically part of the notarization. Review privacy concerns before attaching financial documents, and provide only what the receiving organization requires through an appropriate secure method.

Step-by-step process

  1. Identify the correct form. Determine whether the recipient wants an official form, a support letter, or a sworn affidavit.
  2. Collect accurate facts. Gather names, relationship details, support amounts, dates, and requested evidence.
  3. Draft clearly. State who is providing support, to whom, for what purpose, and for how long.
  4. Verify the notarial act. Ask whether the signature must be sworn, acknowledged, witnessed, or simply signed.
  5. Do not sign early. Keep the signature blank when the notary must supervise the act.
  6. Complete the online session. Upload the final document, verify identity, and sign when instructed.
  7. Submit the full package. Attach required evidence and follow the recipient’s delivery instructions.

What should be reviewed before signing?

Check all financial numbers, dates, names, addresses, and statements about employment or household support. Avoid estimates presented as exact facts. Make clear whether support is ongoing, temporary, or limited to a particular expense. Do not include promises you do not understand or cannot fulfill.

Official support forms can create important obligations. Read the form instructions and obtain professional advice when the consequences are unclear. SafeNotarize provides notarization and general document information, not legal, immigration, tax, or financial advice.

When is notarization commonly requested?

A school, housing provider, government office, financial institution, immigration-related recipient, or another organization may request a notarized affidavit to confirm the sponsor’s identity and sworn statement. Requirements vary, so confirm the required form, wording, evidence, and notarial act before signing.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Using an affidavit when the recipient requires its own official form.
  • Leaving the amount, duration, relationship, or beneficiary unclear.
  • Referring to supporting evidence without attaching or identifying it.
  • Signing before the notary when a sworn signature must occur during the session.

Frequently asked questions

Is an affidavit of support the same as a financial support letter?

They can overlap, but an affidavit is usually a sworn or formally acknowledged statement, while a support letter may be an informal signed explanation. The recipient decides which format is required.

Do immigration support forms always need notarization?

No. Official immigration forms have their own signature and evidence rules, and some are signed without notarization. Use the current official instructions rather than adding a notary certificate unnecessarily.

Can a notary verify my income?

No. A notary verifies identity and performs the requested notarial act. The notary does not audit financial records or certify that income is accurate or sufficient.

Can supporting evidence be attached after notarization?

Often it can, but the recipient may require exhibits to be identified in the affidavit or presented as one package. Confirm the required format before the session.

Can two supporters sign one affidavit?

Possibly. If the document permits multiple signers, each person must complete the required identity and signing process. Separate affidavits may be clearer when supporters provide different information.

Can I use remote online notarization for a consulate or foreign country?

Acceptance varies. Ask the consulate, foreign agency, or receiving party whether it accepts an electronically signed and remotely notarized document and whether authentication is also needed.

Does notarization make the support promise enforceable?

Notarization alone does not determine legal enforceability. It verifies the signing act. The wording, law, official form, and surrounding circumstances determine the document’s legal effect.
