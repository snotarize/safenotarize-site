Create a Vehicle Release Authorization Letter

A vehicle release authorization gives a named person permission to pick up or receive a vehicle from a tow yard, impound lot, repair shop, dealership, or another holder.

When this document may be useful

  • The registered owner cannot pick up the vehicle personally.
  • A tow yard or impound lot requires written authorization.
  • A repair shop or dealer needs permission to release the vehicle.
  • Another person is collecting keys, records, or the vehicle for the owner.

Information to have ready

  • Vehicle owner’s full name
  • Authorized person’s full name
  • Vehicle year, make, and model
  • VIN or license plate number
  • Name of the tow yard, shop, dealer, or holder
  • Any release limits or special instructions

Does this document need notarization?

That depends on the receiving organization and the purpose of the document. Some recipients request a notarized signature, while others accept a signed document without notarization. Confirm the recipient’s requirements before your session.

Common questions

Can I edit the document before downloading it?

Yes. Review the preview and update your answers before generating the final PDF.

Should I sign before meeting the notary?

Do not sign in advance when the receiving organization or notarial act requires the notary to witness the signature. Leave the signature area blank unless you have confirmed otherwise.

Can I use this template in every state or country?

The template is provided for general convenience. Requirements vary by recipient, location, and situation, so confirm acceptance before using it.
