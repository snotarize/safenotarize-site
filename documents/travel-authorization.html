Travel Authorization Letter

Use this guided template to authorize another person to travel for a stated destination, period, and purpose. Review the completed document and confirm that it meets the receiving organization’s requirements before notarization.

Common uses

  • Providing a clear written statement or authorization.
  • Supporting a request from a school, business, agency, or service provider.
  • Creating a signed record that may require acknowledgment or an oath.

Before your notary session

  • Confirm the recipient accepts a general template.
  • Review names, dates, addresses, and identification details carefully.
  • Do not sign until you are connected with the notary unless instructed otherwise.

Who should use this document?

  • An adult or organization authorizing another person to handle a defined travel-related task, reservation, document, vehicle, or arrangement.
  • A recipient that accepts a general travel authorization rather than requiring a child consent form, power of attorney, company letter, or proprietary form.

When is notarization commonly requested?

Airlines, travel agencies, border authorities, employers, schools, vehicle providers, and other recipients may request notarization. A child traveling without a parent usually needs a child-specific consent document rather than a general travel authorization.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Using a general travel letter for a child-consent situation.
  • Leaving dates, destinations, travelers, tasks, or limits incomplete.
  • Failing to confirm airline, border, employer, or destination requirements.
  • Assuming notarization creates immigration, custody, or entry rights.

Frequently asked questions

Is this the correct document for a child traveling without both parents?

A child-specific travel consent is usually the more relevant document. Confirm the airline, border, custody, and destination requirements.

Can a travel authorization cover several trips?

It may, if the recipient accepts it and the dates, destinations, tasks, and limits are clear. Some authorities prefer a trip-specific document.

Important: This is a general template, not legal advice. Some organizations require their own form or specific wording.
