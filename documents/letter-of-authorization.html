Create a Letter of Authorization Online

A letter of authorization gives another person permission to complete a specific task or act on the signer’s behalf within stated limits.

How this SafeNotarize template works
  1. Complete the guided form.
  2. Review the generated document carefully.
  3. Download the PDF to your device.
  4. Continue to the signer and session intake, then upload the PDF when prompted.
  5. Meet a live online notary when you are ready.

When this document may be useful

  • Picking up documents, property, records, or personal items.
  • Communicating with an organization on the signer’s behalf.
  • Completing a limited transaction or administrative task.
  • Providing written permission when an organization requests it.

Information to have ready

  • Name of the person granting authority
  • Name of the authorized person
  • The exact action being authorized
  • Start and end dates when applicable
  • Limits, conditions, or excluded actions
  • The receiving organization

Does this document need notarization?

That depends on the receiving organization and the purpose of the document. Some recipients request a notarized signature, while others accept a signed document without notarization. Confirm the recipient’s requirements before your session.

Common questions

Can I edit the document before downloading it?

Yes. Review the preview and update your answers before generating the final PDF.

Should I sign before meeting the notary?

Do not sign in advance when the receiving organization or notarial act requires the notary to witness the signature. Leave the signature area blank unless you have confirmed otherwise.

Can I use this template in every state or country?

The template is provided for general convenience. Requirements vary by recipient, location, and situation, so confirm acceptance before using it.

Important: SafeNotarize is not a law firm and does not provide legal advice. This is a general template and informational guide. You are responsible for confirming that the document meets the receiving organization’s requirements.
