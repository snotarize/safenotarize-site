USPS Form 1583 for a Private or Virtual Mailbox

PS Form 1583 authorizes a commercial mail receiving agency or virtual mailbox provider to receive mail on the applicant’s behalf.

Get Official PS Form 1583 PDFI Already Have the Completed PDFBack to Document Library

Quick process

  • Open and complete the official form.
  • Do not sign the section that must be completed before the notary.
  • Continue to the SafeNotarize signer and session intake.
  • Complete the signer and session intake, then upload the form in the secure upload step.

When this form is used

  • Opening a private mailbox with a commercial mail receiving agency.
  • Starting service with a virtual mailbox provider.
  • Authorizing another individual to receive or collect mail when applicable.
  • Updating information after required details change.

What to have ready

  • The mailbox provider’s address and PMB number.
  • The applicant’s full name, contact details, and permanent home address.
  • One valid government-issued photo ID.
  • A second current document that verifies the home address.
  • Business information or an authorized individual’s information when applicable.

Important completion notes

  • Complete all entries requested by the mailbox provider.
  • The applicant must sign or confirm the signature in real-time presence of the CMRA or a qualified notary.
  • Copies of the required photo and address identification are generally attached.
  • The June 2024 form requires separate adult applications for residential or personal use.

Make sure the PDF is upright before continuing

  • Download the official PDF and open it before completing your request.
  • Confirm that both pages display upright and are easy to read.
  • If a page appears upside down, rotate it and use Print → Save as PDF to create a new permanently upright copy.
  • Complete the form, save the corrected PDF, and continue through the SafeNotarize process.
Important: SafeNotarize is not affiliated with the U.S. Postal Service or your mailbox provider. Confirm the provider’s process and identification requirements before the session.
