What you need

Before your POA notary session

  • Your completed Power of Attorney document, preferably as a PDF.
  • A valid, unexpired government-issued photo ID.
  • A phone, tablet, or computer with camera and microphone.
  • A stable internet connection.
  • Any additional signer or witness information, if your document requires it.
Important note

Do not rush the signing step

If your Power of Attorney needs to be signed in front of the notary, wait until your live session before signing. If your document has special instructions, bring them to the session.

Power of Attorney Notary FAQ

Answers to common questions before booking your online POA notary session.

Can I notarize a Power of Attorney online?

Many Power of Attorney documents can be notarized online if the signer meets identity verification and platform requirements. The receiving agency may have its own rules, so confirm with them if you are unsure.

Do I need to upload the Power of Attorney before the session?

Yes. After booking, you should upload the completed document so it can be reviewed and prepared before your session.

What ID do I need?

You need a valid, unexpired government-issued photo ID. Examples may include a driver license, state ID, or passport, depending on verification requirements.

Can SafeNotarize create a Power of Attorney form for me?

No. SafeNotarize provides notary services. We do not prepare legal documents or give legal advice. Please bring your completed document to the session.

What if my Power of Attorney requires witnesses?

Let us know before your session. Witness availability may need to be arranged in advance and may include an additional fee.
