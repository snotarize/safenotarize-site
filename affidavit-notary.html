What you need

Before your online notary session

  • Your completed affidavit or sworn statement, preferably as a PDF.
  • A valid, unexpired government-issued photo ID.
  • A phone, tablet, or computer with camera and microphone.
  • A stable internet connection.
  • A separate email address for each additional signer, if there are multiple signers.
Important note

Do not sign too early if your affidavit requires an oath

Many affidavits use jurat wording, which may require you to swear or affirm that the statement is true and sign during the live notary session.

Affidavit Notary FAQ

Answers to common questions before booking your online affidavit notary session.

Can I notarize an affidavit online?

Many affidavits can be notarized online if the document is eligible and the signer can complete the required identity verification through the platform.

How do I start?

Choose a time using the booking calendar, then upload your affidavit after booking. SafeNotarize will send your secure session email invite before your appointment.

Should I sign my affidavit before the session?

If your affidavit requires an oath or jurat, you may need to sign during the live session. To avoid issues, wait for the notary’s instruction before signing.

What ID do I need?

You need a valid, unexpired government-issued photo ID. Examples may include a driver license, state ID, or passport, depending on verification requirements.

Can you prepare my affidavit for me?

No. SafeNotarize provides online notary services, but we do not draft legal documents or give legal advice. Your affidavit should be prepared before the session.
